LinearDungeonDelve

Due to unity WebASM conversion issues, input breaks upon death or victory.


LinearDungeonDelve (working title) was my sophomore year DES212 combat simulation project. The project revolved around making a balanced combat simulation through automated testing (AI players) and telemetry generation; I'm fairly pleased with how it turned out system-wise, and with a bot making reasonable choices at 60 actions per minute, it should be theoretically balanced. Still, in the playtesting, there were several issues converting from AI to human players that would naturally have to be resolved if I were to try to expand this project further. Namely, players have been having issues understanding the UI without a tutorial, and while the AI can objectively analyze the zones' levels of danger, human players seemed to get overwhelmed when confronted with large walls of red bars, even though they theoretically can be weaved through at human pace.
